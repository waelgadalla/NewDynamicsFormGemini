Idea #5: Self-Hosted Feature Flags for .NET

Product Name: "FeatureGate" or "FlagShip.NET" | Viability Rank: #5 of 6 | Analysis Date: December 2025

Executive Summary

Viability Score: 6.5/10

The Opportunity: LaunchDarkly costs $10K+/year. Open source alternatives (Unleash, Flagsmith) are complex or cloud-focused. .NET shops want self-hosted, native integration, simple setup, no per-seat pricing.

The Product: A NuGet package + optional admin UI for feature flags with targeting rules, A/B testing, gradual rollouts, audit logging. Self-hosted, flat pricing.

Revenue Target: $150K/year difficult - likely capped around $100-150K due to price pressure and commoditization.

Why #5: Simplest to build, but commoditized market with open source alternatives and low price points. LaunchDarkly is too expensive, but free alternatives exist.

The Simplicity Advantage

This is the easiest product to build on the list:

  • Core functionality is straightforward (flag checks, targeting rules)
  • No complex UI design (compared to form builder)
  • Well-understood problem space
  • Microsoft already has IFeatureManagement - you're building on top

Warning: Simplicity means low barrier to entry for competitors too.

1. The Problem

1.1 Why Feature Flags?

Feature flags enable:

  • Trunk-based development: Merge incomplete features, enable when ready
  • Gradual rollouts: Release to 1%, then 10%, then 100%
  • A/B testing: Compare variants with real users
  • Kill switches: Disable features instantly if problems occur
  • Customer segmentation: Beta features for specific customers

1.2 Current Solutions

1.3 The Gap

What .NET developers want: Simple NuGet package, self-hosted, works with existing .NET config, nice admin UI, reasonable flat pricing, no Docker/Kubernetes requirement.

7. Honest Assessment

Why This Is Ranked #5:
  1. Commoditized market: Feature flags are well-understood; many solutions exist
  2. Open source pressure: Unleash and Flagsmith are free and capable
  3. Low price ceiling: Hard to charge more than $200-600 when alternatives are free
  4. Low switching cost: Feature flags are easy to abstract, customers can switch
  5. Volume business: Need hundreds of customers for meaningful revenue
When This Makes Sense:
  • Quick win: Fastest to build, quickest to market
  • Complement: Bundle with other products (DynamicForms, Admin Generator)
  • Learning: Good first product to learn the market
  • Niche: Focus hard on self-hosted .NET government customers

8. Recommended Approach

If you build this, build it as a complement, not a standalone business:

  1. Bundle with Admin Generator or DynamicForms: Free feature flags with paid product purchase
  2. Keep it simple: Don't over-engineer. The whole value is simplicity.
  3. Build in 4-6 weeks: Any longer and you're over-investing
  4. Low price, high volume: $99-199/year, focus on getting many customers
  5. Open source the core? Consider MIT license for SDK, paid admin UI
